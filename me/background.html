Early Life and Origins

I was born and raised in Seoul, South Korea, where I spent my formative years surrounded by a rich cultural heritage and rapidly evolving technology landscape. Growing up in one of Asia's most dynamic cities shaped my perspective on innovation and tradition.

Educational Journey

I began my education in Seoul, South Korea at a local elementary school. Then, my family and I moved to Singapore, where I studied at an internationl school called Singapore American School. I came back to South Korea after 5 years, and spent the rest of middle and high school at Seoul Foreign School. I am currently studying at New York University, where I'm exploring various fields including buisness.

The transition from my previous educational systems to university life has been both challenging and rewarding, offering me unique perspectives on different learning approaches.

Family Background

I have a family of 5. Mom, Dad, older sister, and a younger sister. Yes, I am a middle child :(.

Cultural Identity

Being Korean-American has given me the opportunity to bridge two cultures. I appreciate both the collective mindset and respect for elders from Korean culture, as well as the individualism and innovation encouraged in American society.
